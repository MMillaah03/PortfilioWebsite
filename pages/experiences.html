The World as a Classroom

Experiences That Shape Me

I believe the best tech developers are curious about the world beyond their screens. Traveling, experiencing different cultures, and stepping outside my comfort zone has given me a much broader perspective; one that has impacted how I approach problems, come up with solutions, and collaborate with others.

Below are some of the places I've been, things I've done, and lessons I've carried back. Each one has shaped who I am as a person and as a technologist.

🌍

Places I've Been

Travel & Adventures

🇩🇴

Dominican Republic

Caribbean · 2005

Group Travel with Friends

I went with 2 good friends of mine on a trip to the Dominican Republic to enjoy a nice break in a nice villa with a pool and someone who cooked us breakfast every morning. One of my friends had never traveled outside the US except for a quick trip to Montreal once, so it was a big deal for him. We enjoyed an amazing resort with great hospitality and also got off the resort a bit to do a 4x4 tour and to see a bit more of the country outside of the tourist resorts and see the struggle and poverty many Dominican citizens face.

Culture Food Architecture
🇮🇶

Iraq

Middle East · 2004-2006

Two Military Tours

I did two back-to-back year-long deployments to Iraq working as a military trainer/advisor to the Iraqi Security Forces (Military, Police, Disaster Police/Fire Services, and Border Patrol). During these deployments, I lived with and trained daily with these units and often went out on missions with them as an embedded member of their unit. I drove and literally walked the streets of Baghdad, learning of the rich history and being a part of history as their nation was rebuilding after the fall of Saddam. I learned some basic Arabic, but most of their officers spoke english so it wasn't too bad; sometimes it developed into communicating by hand gestures and training by monkey-see, monkey-do. My main translator later moved to the US and came to visit me in Boston to meet my family.

Education Language Growth
🇹🇿

Tanzania & Zanzibar

Africa · 2013

Safari & Kilimanjaro Adventure

I traveled here alone as my R&R break from my Afghanistan deployment. Through a "travel buddy" website, I met up with another American who was taking a break from working with the Peace Corps in Senegal; we did a Kilimanjaro Climb package together. I then went off on my own Serengeti Safari, joining up with a British couple and two Denmark friends traveling together in an old beat-up landrover adventure vehicle with two young local guides. The Serengeti and Ngorongoro conservation area were amazing. I followed this up with a long weekend stay in Tanzania at a very nice small private resort on the beach that had a lot of regular visitors who all took me under their wings during meal times by asking me to join them at their tables; I did some scuba-diving along the coral reef and even saw a few sharks. I would absolutely love to go back.

Nature Community Adventure
🇵🇪

Peru

South America · 2008

Volunteer Group Team Leader

I volunteered as a team leader to take a large group of high school kids to Peru from a local parish where I used to work as a youth mentor. A close friend of mine was the youth group's coordinator so I would fairly regularly assist on special days and big events. He asked me to assist in leading this service trip (I had already been all over South America as a Marine, so I think it helped assure some of the anxious kids and parents). The kids (and leaders) put a lot of effort into making a positive impact on the little fishing village where we assisted at a school... and that experience also had a big impact on those kids. Two moved to Peru for quite some time with one getting married there, and eight of the kids went into jobs of service specifically (be it military, priesthood, nursing or teaching). We are all connected all around the globe and it was great to be a part of something like that for such young kids.
